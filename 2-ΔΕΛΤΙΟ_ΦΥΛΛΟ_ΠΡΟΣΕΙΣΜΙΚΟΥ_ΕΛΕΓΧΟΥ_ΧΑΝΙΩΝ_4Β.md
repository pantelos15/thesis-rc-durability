## Structural Components
- Check for visible cracks in walls, ceilings, and beams.
- Inspect the foundation for any signs of settlement or movement.
- Evaluate the condition of load-bearing walls.
- Check the integrity of floors and ceilings.
- Assess the condition of staircases and ramps.

## Non-Structural Components
- Inspect windows and doors for proper operation and sealing.
- Check for loose roofing materials.
- Evaluate the condition of external cladding and finishes.
- Ensure that non-structural elements, such as furniture and equipment, are secured.

## Utilities
- Inspect gas, water, and electrical systems for damage.
- Ensure that emergency gas shut-off valves are accessible and operational.
- Check that electrical panels and wiring are in good condition.
- Verify that fire alarm and sprinkler systems are operational.

## Site Conditions
- Evaluate the site for potential hazards, such as unstable ground or nearby structures that could collapse.
- Check drainage systems to ensure proper water flow.
- Assess vegetation and trees that might pose a risk in the event of an earthquake.

## Emergency Preparedness
- Ensure that emergency contact information is available and up-to-date.
- Check for the presence of emergency supplies and equipment.
- Review the building's evacuation plan.

## Post-Inspection Actions
- Document all findings and recommendations for repairs or further evaluations.
- Schedule follow-up inspections based on the severity of findings.

---
**Note:** This checklist serves as a guide and should be adapted based on specific building characteristics and local regulations.
